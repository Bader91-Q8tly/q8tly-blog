Some salons sell the buzz — the crowd at the door, the music up, the sense that something is always happening. **Anosha Beauty Salon** sells the opposite, and does it well. Tucked on the third floor in **Sabah Al-Salem**, this **women-only** salon runs entirely **by appointment**, and that one decision quietly shapes everything else: no waiting around, no crowd, no rush. You book a time, you arrive, and the place is yours.

It is, simply, calm — and spotless.

## What stood out

The first thing you notice is how _uncrowded_ it feels. Because every visit is booked, the salon never tips into the commotion you learn to tolerate elsewhere. The music stays low — the kind that actually lets you switch off rather than the kind you have to talk over.

The space itself is genuinely refined. The detailing is considered, not just clean: warm wood, soft lighting, a wall of nail colours arranged like a paint box, and a row of reclining wash chairs that make the basin step feel like part of the treatment rather than a chore.

[[image:inline-1|The hair-wash lounge — reclining chairs and a full professional product shelf.]]

> Honestly amazing — so tidy and clean. The kind of place you go when you'd rather not wait around.

What our visit kept coming back to, though, wasn't the polish — it was the **attention**. Staff checking in on how comfortable you were, asking what you actually needed, with a warmth in the hospitality that you don't always get. You're offered a coffee and water when you sit down, and it sets the tone for the whole appointment.

[[image:inline-3|A welcome coffee and water on arrival.]]

## A small thing worth mentioning

There's no dedicated prayer room, but when asked, the staff went out of their way: they set up a private, screened space and provided a prayer thobe, a mat, and marked the qibla direction. It's the kind of accommodate-on-request hospitality that tells you how a place treats its guests — offered without fuss.
