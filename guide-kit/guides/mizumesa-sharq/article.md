Some [restaurants](/places/category/restaurants/) hand you a single cuisine. **MizuMesa** hands you the meeting point of two. Tucked at the foot of **KIPCO Tower** in **[Sharq](/places/sharq/)**, it cooks **Nikkei** — the food that was born when Japanese migrants settled in Peru and folded its ingredients into their own technique. The name says it plainly: **mizu** is "water" in Japanese, **mesa** is "table" in Spanish. A Japanese kitchen, a Peruvian pantry, one table.

It is, simply, one of the more considered rooms in the city — and the cooking lives up to it.

## The room

MizuMesa is built to be looked at. The space runs on greenery, low warm lighting and a lot of texture: deep green velvet banquettes, pale bouclé chairs with brass trim, copper-toned tables, and wall niches lit like little shrines. Plants soften every corner, and the lighting is pitched for a long, unhurried evening rather than a quick turnover. It reads as genuinely upscale without tipping into stiff — the kind of place that works equally for a date or a celebration.

[[image:inline-1|Green velvet banquettes, bouclé chairs and greenery under warm light — the dining room at MizuMesa.]]

## The food: Nikkei, properly

The kitchen keeps Japanese discipline at its core and lets Peru bring the colour — aji sauces, citrus, chillies and crunch. The menu rewards ordering across it: charred edamame and rock-shrimp tempura to start, then the rolls, where a dragon-style avocado roll arrives glossed with spicy aji and microgreens, as pretty as it is sharp.

[[image:inline-2|A dragon-style avocado roll finished with aji and microgreens — Japanese form, Peruvian heat.]]

> A Japanese kitchen, a Peruvian pantry, and a beef course you'll be thinking about on the drive home.

## Don't miss the beef

If you order one thing, make it the **beef**. However it's plated on the night — we had it as tender cubes set on crisp rice under a slick of aji amarillo — it comes out rich, deeply savoury and almost startlingly tender, the dish the whole table ends up reaching for. It's the clearest argument for what Nikkei does well: Japanese precision, Peruvian boldness, on one plate.

[[image:inline-3|The standout: tender beef on crisp rice, finished with aji amarillo on a MizuMesa-marked board.]]

## Good to know

MizuMesa sits firmly in the **upscale** bracket — reckon on around **13 KD a head** — and it earns it with service to match: attentive without hovering, quick to check in and clear plates. It's open for both **lunch and dinner**, and while **walk-ins are welcome** (there's a small waiting area by the door), **booking ahead is the better move** — you can reserve a table online at [mizumesa.com](https://mizumesa.com/index.html). There's a **family section** and it handles **groups of six or more** comfortably. Valet isn't offered and street parking is limited — the easy move is to park inside **KIPCO Tower** and walk over.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is Nikkei cuisine?

Nikkei is the fusion of Japanese and Peruvian cooking, born when Japanese migrants settled in Peru in the late 19th century. At MizuMesa it means Japanese technique built on Peruvian ingredients and flavours — aji sauces, citrus and chillies alongside sushi, tempura and the like.

### What should I order at MizuMesa?

Order across the menu — edamame and rock-shrimp tempura, a roll or two — but don't skip the **beef**, which is the standout: tender, rich and the dish the table keeps coming back to.

### Do I need a reservation?

It's recommended. MizuMesa is upscale and popular, so booking ahead is the safe move — you can reserve a table online at [mizumesa.com](https://mizumesa.com/index.html). Walk-ins are welcome too, with a small waiting area by the door.

### Where is MizuMesa, and when is it open?

It's in Sharq on Khalid Ibn Al-Waleed Street, by KIPCO Tower, open for lunch and dinner from 1:00 PM to 11:00 PM. To book, reserve online at [mizumesa.com](https://mizumesa.com/index.html) or call +965 2207 0777.

### Is MizuMesa good for groups or families?

Yes — there's a family section and it comfortably seats groups of six or more. Parking is easiest inside KIPCO Tower.
