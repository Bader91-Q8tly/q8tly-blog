Some [restaurants](/places/category/restaurants/) make you feel like you're passing through. **Naranj** is built for staying. Step through the arched doorway in **[Salmiya](/places/salmiya/)** and the room opens into something closer to an old Damascus courtyard — warm wood, rows of hanging lanterns, a planted green wall, and the soft sound of an indoor fountain. The tables are generous and well spaced, the lighting is low, and the whole place is pitched at one thing: a long, unhurried evening.

It is, simply, a room that makes dinner feel like an occasion.

## The room

Naranj leans fully into old-Syrian design and pulls it off. Ornate metal lanterns hang in rows, arched windows look onto a planted interior garden, and a fountain anchors the space so it never feels flat or canteen-like. Tables sit far enough apart that the conversation stays yours. It reads as considered rather than themed — which is exactly why it lands so well for a date night.

[[image:inline-1|Hanging lanterns and arched windows over the planted interior garden.]]

## The food

The kitchen is Syrian, and the mezze is where to start — tabbouleh, labneh and the raw-meat plates arrive looking the part, with warm bread and good olive oil alongside. The standout, though, is the **meat arayes**: bread crisped around a seasoned meat filling, the kind of order everyone reaches across the table for. If you build a visit around one dish, make it that one.

[[image:inline-2|A Syrian mezze spread — tabbouleh, raw-meat plates and the standout meat arayes with warm bread.]]

> An old-Damascus room, attentive waiters, and a dessert you didn't order — Naranj is built for a long evening, not a quick bite.

## Service, and a sweet ending

Service is a genuine strength here: attentive, eager waiters who check in without hovering and happily steer you through the menu when asked. And the meal closes the way the best Levantine tables do — with a **complimentary dessert** sent out unprompted, a tray of fruit and Arabic sweets that turns the end of dinner into its own small event.

[[image:inline-3|The complimentary dessert — watermelon and a tray of Arabic sweets to close the meal.]]

## Good to know

Naranj serves **shisha** and has both indoor and outdoor seating, so you can settle in after the plates are cleared; note there's a smoking section, so plan accordingly if you'd rather avoid it. **Valet parking** takes the hassle out of arriving on the Salmiya strip. Pricing sits in the upper-mid range — this is a take-your-time, sit-down dinner rather than a quick bite.

## Frequently asked questions

### What should I order at Naranj?

Start with the Syrian mezze and make sure the **meat arayes** is on the table — it's the standout. Save room, too: the meal ends with a complimentary dessert.

### Does Naranj have outdoor seating and shisha?

Yes — there's both indoor and outdoor seating, and shisha is served. There's a dedicated smoking section, so factor that in.

### Is Naranj good for a date night?

It's one of its strengths. The old-Damascus room, low lighting, indoor fountain and well-spaced tables make it an easy pick for a relaxed, special-feeling dinner.

### Where is Naranj, and when is it open?

Naranj is on Arabian Gulf Street in Salmiya, open daily from 9:00 AM to midnight, with last orders at 11:30 PM. Call +965 2226 8666 to book.
