Some [salons](/places/category/salons/) make you pick a lane — the polished nail bar, the serious hair chair, or the [spa](/places/category/spas-massage/) room down the hall. **South Avenue — salon & spa for ladies** would rather you didn't choose. On the **second floor** of a building in **[Sabah Al-Salem](/places/sabah-al-salem/)**, this **women-only** salon packs the full range — hair, nails, skin and a deep bench of natural treatments — onto one calm, well-stocked floor, and keeps the prices sensible enough that it becomes the regular rather than the splurge.

It is, simply, the kind of place you end up going back to.

## What stood out

The first thing that lands is how _organised_ it is. Everything has its place: a long wall of nail colour arranged like a paint box, lit shelves of recognisable product lines, and a lounge that's genuinely comfortable to wait in. It reads as clean in the way that tells you someone resets the room between clients, not just at the end of the day — and the air conditioning is doing serious work, which matters more than anyone admits in a Kuwait summer.

[[image:inline-1|The lounge and product wall — lit shelves, a wall of polish, and somewhere comfortable to sit.]]

The other thing regulars keep coming back to is the **wait — or the lack of one**. Appointments don't pile up here, so you're seen close to your time and back out without the afternoon disappearing. The staff are friendly and detail-focused, and the work — hair, nails, treatments — comes back neat. None of it is showy; it's just consistent, which is exactly what quietly turns a salon into _your_ salon.

> If you go, you'll always come back.

## The nail bar

Nails are clearly a point of pride. A full wall of **ORLY** shades, with **essie** at the stations, means you're picking from the actual range rather than a tired tray of leftovers — the colour wall alone is worth the trip if you're the type who changes your mind three times before settling.

[[image:inline-2|A full wall of ORLY polish, arranged like a paint box — pick from the range, not the leftovers.]]

## Natural treatments, not just blow-dries

Where South Avenue goes a step deeper than the average neighbourhood salon is its **natural-treatment menu**. Alongside the usual cuts, colour and blow-dries, there's a long list of herbal hair masks — coconut, coffee, rice, butter, moringa, even henna — plus scalp oils, sold per session or in cheaper multi-session packages. It's the kind of menu you book when you want to treat your hair over a few weeks rather than fix it in one sitting. The body shelves carry names you'll know — LUSH, L'Occitane, Rituals — so the products on the trolley aren't an afterthought either.

Pricing sits comfortably in **mid-range, value territory**: individual treatments start low and the multi-session packages bring the per-visit cost down further. Treat any number as indicative and confirm it when you book.

[[image:inline-3|Lit shelves of recognisable body and hair lines — LUSH, L'Occitane and Rituals among them.]]

## Who it's for

South Avenue suits the person who'd rather not split a getting-ready day across three different places, and who values a quick, no-fuss booking over a big-name fuss.

### Best for

- Anyone who wants hair, nails and treatments under one roof — and out the door on time.
- Groups getting ready together — it handles parties of six or more comfortably.
- A standing natural-treatment routine — herbal hair masks and scalp oils by the package.
- Stay-at-home days — there's an at-home service line, too.

Yes — it's a salon & spa for ladies.

### Do I need an appointment?

Booking is recommended, but waits tend to be short. Call **5026 8000** or **5038 1000** for the Block 1 branch to reserve a slot.

### What treatments does South Avenue offer?

Hair (cuts, colour, blow-dries), nails (ORLY and essie), skin and body care, plus a natural-treatment menu of herbal hair masks — coconut, coffee, rice, butter, moringa, henna — and scalp oils, sold per session or in multi-session packages.

### Does South Avenue do home visits?

Yes — there's a dedicated at-home service line (**5004 0004**), running daily from 10:00 AM.

### Where is South Avenue, and when is it open?

It's on the second floor in Sabah Al-Salem, with branches in Block 1 and Block 3, open daily from 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM (Eid timings differ).
